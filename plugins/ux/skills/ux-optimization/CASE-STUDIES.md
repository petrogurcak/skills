## üéØ Pattern Recognition: What Works

### Universal Truths (Work Across All Case Studies)

1. **Simplification Always Wins**
   - Remove fields: +10-30% every time
   - Remove steps: +15-40% completion
   - Remove choices: +20-35% decision rate

2. **Mobile Is Not Optional**
   - 60%+ traffic from mobile
   - Mobile UX must be designed separately
   - Thumb zone placement critical

3. **Trust Signals Matter**
   - Security badges: +5-12%
   - Customer count: +8-15%
   - Reviews/testimonials: +10-20%

4. **A/B Testing Is Mandatory**
   - 50% of changes hurt metrics
   - "Obvious" improvements often fail
   - Data > opinions, always

5. **Progressive Disclosure Works**
   - Show essentials first
   - Advanced options hidden but accessible
   - Completion rates +25-45%

### Context-Dependent (Test in Your Case)

1. **Color Psychology**
   - Orange vs green vs red CTAs
   - Varies by industry and brand
   - Always A/B test

2. **Social Proof Type**
   - Some audiences trust numbers
   - Others prefer testimonials
   - B2B vs B2C different

3. **Pricing Display**
   - Show discount or not?
   - Show VAT included/excluded?
   - Context matters

## üö´ What Doesn't Work (Failed Tests)

### Documented Failures from Case Studies

1. **Too Much Personalization**
   - One client: personalized everything
   - Result: -12% conversion (felt creepy)
   - Learning: Subtle personalization better

2. **Gamification Without Purpose**
   - Added points/badges to checkout
   - Result: -8% completion (distraction)
   - Learning: Don't gamify purchase flow

3. **Auto-playing Videos**
   - Product videos that auto-play
   - Result: -15% page engagement
   - Learning: User control > automation

4. **Too Many Options**
   - Offered 5 shipping options
   - Result: -18% checkout completion
   - Learning: 2-3 options optimal

5. **Premature Upsells**
   - Upsell before adding to cart
   - Result: -22% add-to-cart rate
   - Learning: Upsell in cart, not before

### Case Study #21: Pricing Psychology - 18 Tips (Dima Melnik)

**Expert:** Dima Melnik (Pricing strategy consultant)

**Proven Results:**
- **Increased premium option acceptance: 11% ‚Üí 52%** (+373% improvement)
- **85% of clients moved to expensive options** (logistics business)
- **Sold product for 10√ó higher price** to large portion of clients (SaaS)
- **Middle option adoption: 85%**, cheapest only 10%

**Core Philosophy:**
"One-fit-all" pricing = rejections or underpricing. Maximize acceptance rate through psychological pricing techniques.

**18 Actionable Techniques:**

**1. Segment-Specific Offers ("This was prepared for me")**
- Different buyer segments = different needs
- Museum: $/visitor, Call center: $/operator, Radio: $/minute
- Tailored metrics, packages, and prices per segment
- Result: Personalization increases perceived value

**2. Multiple Paid Options ("More shots = more chances")**
- Single price never optimal (too expensive OR suspiciously cheap)
- Offer 3-4 paid tiers to capture different willingness to pay
- Warning: Free + Paid = only 1 option (not optimal)
- Result: Captures wider customer base

**3. Maximum 4 Packages ("Confused mind doesn't buy")**
- 5+ options = paradox of choice (cognitive overload)
- 4 = max before needing to count
- More segments? Separate offers (Solo vs Firm)
- Result: Reduces decision friction

**4. Default Option Psychology**
- First offer sets benchmark
- $0 first = everything else seems expensive
- Put free/cheapest option BELOW main tiers
- Result: Price-sensitive find it anyway, others anchored higher

**5. Middle Option = Safe Bet**
- People avoid extremes
- Middle = neither cheapest nor most expensive
- **Target KPI: 50-65% choose middle option**
- Result: Predictable revenue, optimal value perception

**6. Substitution Effect (Visual Package Size)**
- Humans substitute simple judgment (# of benefits) for complex (overall value)
- Better product = more checkmarks, larger visual package
- Visual size influences perceived value
- Result: Customers use visual heuristics

**7. Compare Key Features Only**
- Hide common features in detailed table
- Show 3-4 essential common features above packages
- Compare only differentiators
- Example: 6 vs 3 features easier to sell than 33 vs 30
- Result: Simplifies comparison, highlights upgrades

**8. No-Brainer Smart Choice**
- Add small extra value to top tier (+premium support)
- Create LARGE price jump (+200 vs +50)
- Middle becomes obvious "smart choice"
- Result: Drives middle option selection

**9. Seductive Pricing (.9 endings)**
- $199 perceived lower than $200
- Use .9 on options you want to sell (middle + top)
- Cheapest ends in .0 (less attractive)
- **Best result: 85% middle, 10% cheapest**
- Result: +373% proven impact

**10. Start with Highest Price First**
- "Big guns first" sets expensive benchmark
- Other options seem affordable in comparison
- Warning: Don't use if prices vary extremely (‚Ç¨5000 vs ‚Ç¨5)
- Result: Anchors perception high

**11. Package Names = Buyer Personas**
- "Broker, Developer, Investor" not "Basic, Pro, Enterprise"
- Cocktail party effect: your name makes you turn around
- "This is ME!" identification
- Result: Instant relevance, self-selection

**12. Make Price Look Tiny (Typography)**
- Large bold: Product name, discount
- Small font: Actual price
- Price seems less important, cheaper
- Result: Reduces price sensitivity

**13. Dream Outcome Summary**
- Single sentence above pricing: "Visitors with excellent experience thanks to..."
- Focus on outcome, not product
- "This is what I dream about"
- Result: Value-focused decision making

**14. Anchor with High Numbers**
- Show large number before prices
- "3,000,000 calls with superior sound" ‚Üí $399
- Examples: HBO (10,000 entertainment ‚Üí $14.99), Basecamp (1,562 orgs ‚Üí $299)
- Result: Prices seem small by comparison

**15. Art of Attention (Visual Highlighting)**
- Highlighted option = most favorable
- Tools: Color, callout badge, discount display
- "Art schools top choice" label
- Result: Directs decision to preferred tier

**16. Minimize Perceived Cost**
- $99/month better than $1,188/year
- Monthly with annual payment (10-20% premium for true monthly)
- Compare to tiny units: $0.50/week, $0.22/day, $0.006/visitor
- Result: Reduces sticker shock

**17. Crown Jewel Clients (Enterprise Tier)**
- Add logos of famous brands to top tier
- Halo effect: Association with quality
- Proves: 1) Can serve enterprise, 2) Others bought it
- Result: Credibility + social proof

**18. Add-Ons at Checkout**
- Small purchases seem valuable after big expense
- Create add-ons from benefits valuable to subset
- Batteries with electronics example
- Separate checkout page or price list
- Result: Increases AOV

**Implementation Metrics:**
- Premium acceptance: 11% ‚Üí 52% (+373%)
- Middle option selection: Target 50-65%, achieved 85%
- Client migration to expensive options: 85%
- Price realization: 10√ó for large client portion

**Key Learnings:**
- Pricing is psychology, not just math
- Multiple techniques compound impact
- Test and iterate (not all work everywhere)
- Middle tier = sweet spot for most businesses
- Visual presentation matters as much as actual numbers

**B2B Focus:**
- Particularly effective for B2B SaaS
- Segment-specific offers critical
- Outcome-focused messaging essential
- Social proof (logos) highly valuable
