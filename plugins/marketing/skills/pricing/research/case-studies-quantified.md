# Case Studies: Quantified Pricing Impact

Real-world examples with measurable business outcomes.

---

## Success Stories

### Porsche Cayenne — Design Around Price

**Context:** Late 1990s, Porsche entering SUV market (perceived as huge risk)

**Approach:**
- Before development, researched customer WTP and desired features
- Removed features customers didn't value (racing transmission)
- Added features customers would pay for (large cup holders)

**Result:** Cayenne became the most profitable vehicle in industry history, secured Porsche's financial stability.

**Lesson:** Price → Design → Build (not Design → Build → Price)

---

### Gillette Guard — Target Price Design

**Context:** 2009, Gillette had only 22% market share in India (Mach 3 too expensive)

**Approach:**
- Set target price of 15 rupees first
- Designed product backwards from price constraint
- Reduced parts from 25 to just 4

**Result:** 60% market share in India within 2 years

**Lesson:** When you know the price ceiling, engineer to fit it.

---

### Williams-Sonoma Bread Maker — Decoy Effect

**Context:** Initial bread maker at $275 had weak sales

**Approach:**
- Added second, larger model at $429

**Result:**
- $429 model sales: 0
- $275 model sales: **nearly doubled**

**Lesson:** The expensive option made the original look like a great deal.

---

### Dräger — WTP-Driven Product Design

**Context:** Gas detector development with customer process monitoring

**Approach:**
- Spent thousands of hours observing workers in sewers
- Discovered unspoken needs
- Designed product around observed WTP

**Result:** 250% higher revenue than original plan

**Lesson:** Observational research reveals WTP better than surveys.

---

### Internet Startup — Psychological Thresholds

**Context:** SaaS startup optimizing pricing structure

**Approach:**
- Portfolio restructuring
- Applied psychological price thresholds
- Optimized package configuration

**Result:**
- ARPU (Average Revenue Per User): **+36%**
- MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue): **+29%**

---

### SaaS Company — Feature Reduction

**Context:** Product with 27 features causing "feature shock"

**Approach:**
- Identified which features customers actually valued
- Reduced from 27 features to 8 core features

**Result:** Conversion and revenue increased by **more than 25%**

**Lesson:** Less is more when features aren't valued.

---

### Logistics Company — Tier Migration

**Context:** Moving customers to higher-value product variants

**Approach:**
- Implemented new pricing strategies
- Restructured tier offerings

**Result:** **85% of clients** migrated to more expensive variants

## Failure Stories

### Amazon Fire Phone — Feature Shock

**Context:** Amazon's first smartphone

**Problem:**
- Technical perfection prioritized over customer WTP
- Innovative features (3D display) that customers didn't value
- Price didn't match perceived value

**Result:** **$170 million write-off** for unsold inventory

**Lesson:** Innovation without WTP validation = disaster.

---

### Dodge Dart — Missed Predictions

**Context:** Vehicle launch with flawed pricing/positioning

**Result:** Sales reached only **25% (one quarter)** of predicted volume

## Quantified Psychological Effects

### Anchoring — Social Security Experiment

| SSN Last Digits | Average Bid | vs. Baseline |
|-----------------|-------------|--------------|
| 00-19 | $8.64 | Baseline |
| 20-39 | $11.73 | +36% |
| 40-59 | $13.45 | +56% |
| 60-79 | $21.18 | +145% |
| 80-99 | $29.89 | **+246%** |

Maximum impact: **+346%** WTP from random number exposure

---

### Decoy Effect — The Economist

| Scenario | Web Only ($59) | Print ($125) | Print+Web ($125) |
|----------|----------------|--------------|------------------|
| With decoy | 16% | 0% | **84%** |
| Without decoy | 68% | - | 32% |

**Shift:** 52 percentage points toward premium option

---

### Power of FREE — Chocolate Experiment

| Scenario | Lindt Truffle | Hershey's Kiss |
|----------|---------------|----------------|
| Lindt 15¢, Hershey's 1¢ | 73% | 27% |
| Lindt 14¢, Hershey's FREE | 31% | **69%** |

**Shift:** 42 percentage points from superior product to FREE inferior product

---

### Social Pressure — Eyes Effect

**Context:** Honesty box for drinks with picture of eyes above

**Result:** Collections increased **2.76x** when eyes were displayed

## The Leverage Effect of Price

### Price vs. Volume vs. Cost

| Improvement | Impact on Profit |
|-------------|------------------|
| **Price +1%** | **+11.1% profit** |
| Volume +1% | +3.3% profit |
| Variable cost -1% | +7.8% profit |
| Fixed cost -1% | +2.3% profit |

### Illustrative Example

| Change | Profit Impact |
|--------|---------------|
| **Price +5%** | **+50% profit** |
| Volume +5% | +20% profit |

**Insight:** Price is the most powerful profit lever.

## Success Rates

### Companies with Defined Pricing Strategy

- **40% higher chance** of realizing monetization potential
- When led by top management: **33% higher profit** on average

### Innovation Failure Rate

- **72% of all new products** fail to meet financial goals or completely fail
- Root cause: Pricing conversation delayed until after development

---

## Summary: What Separates Winners from Losers

| Winners | Losers |
|---------|--------|
| Price → Design → Build | Design → Build → Price |
| WTP research before development | WTP as afterthought |
| Feature reduction to core value | Feature bloat ("more is more") |
| Psychological pricing applied | Round numbers, no anchoring |
| Customer observation (ethnographic) | Survey-only research |
