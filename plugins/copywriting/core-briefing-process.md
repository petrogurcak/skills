# Core Briefing Process — 8 Questions

Universal pre-writing brief. Run before any copywriting work. Loaded by `web-copy`, `product-copy`, `newsletter`, `ig-content`, `ig-strategy` and others.

**Otto's principle:**

> "Nemohu začít psát texty, dokud nemám jasné zadání."
> _(Cannot begin writing without clear direction.)_

---

## The 8 Questions

| # | Question | What to ask |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **CO** (What) | What are you communicating? Product, service, idea, story? |
| 2 | **JAK** (How) | How does it work? What is the mechanism / process / promise? |
| 3 | **ČÍM** (With what / Differentiator) | What makes you different from competitors? Unique angle? |
| 4 | **KDE** (Where) | Where is the message published? Platform, format, context. |
| 5 | **KOMU** (To whom / Audience) | Who is the target? Demographics, psychographics, jobs-to-be-done, pain points. |
| 6 | **KDO** (Who) | Who is speaking? Brand voice, persona, tone authority. |
| 7 | **KAM** (Where to / Goal) | What action do we want? CTA, conversion, behavior change. |
| 8 | **PROČ** (Why / Purpose) | Why does this content exist? What is the deeper why behind the campaign? |

---

## Quick Brief (4 minimum)

For fast turnaround copy (single IG post, quick email), confirm at minimum these 4:

- **KOMU** — Who are you talking to?
- **KAM** — What action do you want?
- **ČÍM** — Why you / what's the angle?
- **PROČ** — Why this content right now?

If any of the 4 is unknown → **STOP**. Brief first.

---

## Full Brief (8 questions)

Use full 8 for major campaigns: web redesigns, product launches, brand storytelling, multi-channel campaigns.

### Template

```markdown
## Brief: [Campaign / Content name]

**1. CO (What):**
[Product, service, idea being communicated]

**2. JAK (How):**
[Mechanism, process, promise — how it actually works]

**3. ČÍM (Differentiator):**
[Unique angle — what makes this different]

**4. KDE (Platform):**
[IG / web / email / podcast / paid ads / etc.]

**5. KOMU (Audience):**
- Demographics:
- Psychographics:
- Jobs-to-be-done:
- Pain points:
- Objections:

**6. KDO (Speaker):**
[Brand voice, persona, tone]

**7. KAM (Goal):**
[Specific action — DM, click, buy, sign up, share]

**8. PROČ (Purpose):**
[Why now? Deeper why behind the campaign]
```

---

## How to Use

### Phase 1: Detect missing pieces

Read user request. Check which of 8 questions are answered. Mark gaps.

### Phase 2: Prompt user for gaps

For each unanswered question, ask once. Don't interrogate.

**Good:**

> "Brief check:
> - Komu píšeme? (audience)
> - Co má reader udělat? (goal/CTA)
> - Proč tenhle obsah teď? (purpose)
> Bez těchto tří nedokážu napsat dobrý copy."

**Bad:**

> "Tell me about your brand voice. What is your unique value proposition? Who is your target persona? What are their pain points? What is your CTA strategy?..."

### Phase 3: Confirm understanding before writing

Echo back the brief in 3-4 lines. Get user's "yes, write" or correction.

---

## Why This Matters

**Without brief:**

- Generic copy that sounds like everyone else
- Wrong audience targeting
- Weak CTA (or none)
- No emotional resonance
- Multiple revisions needed

**With brief:**

- Specific, targeted copy
- Clear angle (ČÍM)
- Strong CTA aligned with KAM
- Emotional connection via PROČ
- One iteration usually enough

**Otto's law:**

> "Lepší 30 minut briefu a 30 minut psaní, než 0 minut briefu a 5 hodin přepisování."

---

## Common Mistakes

**❌ Skipping briefing:**
"Let me just start writing..." → STOP. Brief first.

**❌ Assuming the brief:**
"They probably want X" → No. Confirm.

**❌ Over-briefing:**
20 questions for a single tweet. Match brief depth to content scope.

**❌ Brief without PROČ:**
Tactical copy without strategic why. Always tag the deeper purpose.
